History

Heesu "Cahaya Cinta" Foundation was established on 11 November 2007 by a South Korean citizen named Poong Nam Choi, and was inaugurated by the Ministry of Social Affairs of Republic of Indonesia on 14 March 2009.

Children in Heesu Foundation have specific needs in order to support their activities and medical conditions, such as physiotherapy, speech therapy, and occupational therapy. Meanwhile, they must always be accompanied by pediatricians, neurologists, orthopedic doctors, medical rehabilitation doctors, therapists, nutritionists, psychologists, special educational needs teachers and other medical personnel.

When first established, Heesu Foundation rehabilitation team was only comprised of a physiotherapist, a nurse and a general practitioner. Novi, the first physiotherapist at Heesu Foundation, served dual function as an occupational therapist and speech therapist to improve the children's ability to eat, exercise/perform activities and speak.

At Heesu, all experts and caregivers are encouraged to create a family atmosphere and sense of caring between individuals. They do not regard their work as burdensome as they do it wholeheartedly. To conclude, Heesu Foundation is made of a group of professionals and volunteers who genuinely care for children with multiple disabilities.

We are very grateful that, besides our employees, there are still many people who care about the children; who have come to support us by conducting social activities, donations, or even just visiting to entertain the children. Our supporters range from individuals, companies, communities, and organizations, either domestic or foreign.

Vision

Become the best and most reliable nursing home for children with multiple disabilities in order to achieve the most optimal empowerment of children with multiple disabilities for them to become more independent physically and socially.

Mission

  • Anticipate the needs of children with multiple disabilities through early detection services
  • Provide thorough rehabilitation services to children with multiple disabilities through physical and occupational therapy programs
  • Guide parents to have sufficient knowledge and right and effective attitude to provide care for children who have multiple disabilities
  • Guide and develop the employees of the foundation for them to be manpower who are dedicated and professional

Objectives

To help neglected children with multiple disabilities or parents who are unable to provide care for their children. Also, to coach and empower children with multiple disabilities, for them to be able to develop the potentials they have.
